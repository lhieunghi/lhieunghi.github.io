Branding Kit

Overview

Branding kit for a local non-profit.

Requirements

Rebrand for a local nonprofit including new logo, font choices, colors, grid systems, image banks, and collateral. Write usage guidelines for all redesigned assets and related content in book format.

The Company:

The San Jose Public Library Foundation is a non-profit organization that works towards increasing funding for local libraries in order to empower, connect, and educate those that need it the most.

My Role:

An independent designer with complete autonomy of the project from start to finish, provided the project requirements are met.

Process:

  1. Create mood boards which define and apply company values to a design language.

    2. I chose a modern and professional approach for my redesign. I also tried to make it warm and inviting so that people would have confidence reaching out for resources and felt good about donating too. Lastly, I tried to give it a bit of a whimsical feel like the excitement one might get from reading a good book so I chose a somewhat dark and vintage color scheme that was still professional looking.

  2. Brainstorm using thumbnails and ideations to choose a suitable design direction.

    3. After many design iterations, I decided on the tree map of San Jose logo. I felt it was memorable and very appropriate for the subject matter since a tree represents knowledge, education, and honorable institutions and a map with branches alludes to connecting people in the community. The bookmark was an extra fun element that alluded more to the books in the library. Finally, for the branding kit, I designed an open book illustration to be included on each page that was visually interesting, but not distracting from the content. Then also created standard and easy to follow design guidelines and matching collateral.

  3. Complete draft with print mockup.
